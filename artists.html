Who Was Vincent van Gogh?

Vincent van Gogh (1853-1890) was a Dutch Post-Impressionist painter whose bold colors and emotional, expressive brushwork went on to influence generations of artists. During his lifetime he was largely unrecognized, struggled with poverty and mental illness, and sold only one painting. Today he is regarded as one of the most important figures in the history of Western art.

Van Gogh did not begin painting seriously until his late twenties. In roughly a decade of work, he produced more than 2,000 pieces of art, including around 860 oil paintings, most of them completed in the final two years of his life.

Life Timeline

Year Event
1853 Born in Groot-Zundert, the Netherlands.
1880 Decides to pursue painting full time after early careers as an art dealer and lay preacher.
1886 Moves to Paris and is exposed to Impressionist art, brightening his color palette.
1888 Moves to Arles in southern France, where he paints Sunflowers and Bedroom in Arles.
1889 Voluntarily enters an asylum in Saint-Remy, where he paints The Starry Night.
1890 Dies in Auvers-sur-Oise, France, at the age of 37.

Why His Work Still Matters

  • His thick, textured brushstrokes (impasto) helped define Post-Impressionism.
  • His use of bold, expressive color influenced later movements such as Fauvism and Expressionism.
  • His personal letters, especially to his brother Theo, give art historians rare insight into an artist's creative process.
  • Museums built around his work, such as the Van Gogh Museum in Amsterdam, continue to draw millions of visitors each year.
"I dream of painting and then I paint my dream." - a sentiment often attributed to van Gogh, reflecting how closely his inner emotional life shaped his art.
