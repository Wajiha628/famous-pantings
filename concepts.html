Oil Painting

Oil painting is a technique that uses pigments mixed with oil. It is known for rich colors and long-lasting artwork.

Post-Impressionism

Post-Impressionism is an art movement that focuses on emotion, symbolism, and bold colors. Vincent van Gogh was one of its most famous artists.

Brushstrokes

Brushstrokes are the marks left by a painter's brush. Van Gogh used thick, expressive brushstrokes to create movement in his paintings.

Composition

Composition refers to how objects are arranged within a painting to create balance and interest.

Perspective

Perspective is a technique artists use to make objects appear closer or farther away, creating depth.

Color Theory

Color theory studies how colors interact and affect mood. Van Gogh often used complementary colors, like the blues and yellows in The Starry Night, to make each hue appear more vivid next to the other.

Impasto

Impasto is a technique where paint is applied so thickly that it stands up off the canvas, sometimes still showing brush or palette knife marks. Van Gogh relied heavily on impasto to give his skies and fields a raised, textured surface.

Chiaroscuro

Chiaroscuro is the use of strong contrasts between light and dark to give a painting a sense of volume and drama. Renaissance artists like Leonardo da Vinci used this technique to make figures appear three-dimensional.

Symbolism

Symbolism in painting is the use of objects, colors, or figures to represent ideas beyond their literal appearance. Edvard Munch's The Scream uses a distorted figure and swirling background to symbolize anxiety and existential dread rather than a literal scene.

Texture

Texture describes the surface quality of a painting, whether smooth and blended or thick and raised. Artists build texture through brushwork, layering, and the amount of paint applied, giving viewers a visual sense of touch even though they can't physically feel the canvas.

Movement in Art

Movement refers to the way an artist guides the viewer's eye through a painting using lines, shapes, and brushstrokes. Van Gogh created a strong sense of movement in The Starry Night through the swirling patterns in the sky.
